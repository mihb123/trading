# Price Action Trading Strategies - Full Guide

By Robert Callahan | October 12, 2025 | 15 min read

## Key Takeaways
- **Core Elements:** Candlestick patterns, support/resistance zones, trendlines
- **Why It Works:** Reflects human psychology, effective in forex and futures
- **Main Benefits:** Simplifies decisions, works across markets, no lagging indicators
- **Key Strategies:** Pin bars (reversal), inside bars (breakout), breakout-retest combinations
- **Advanced Techniques:** Engulfing candles, double tops/bottoms, trendline channels, minimal indicators
- **Risk Management:** Align with market structure, 1-2% risk per trade

## Core Principles

### Multiple Timeframe Analysis
- Higher timeframes (daily/4H) for big picture and key levels
- Lower timeframes (1H/15min) for precise entries
- 3-to-1 ratio: trade 1H, use 4H for context, 15min for entry refinement

### Support and Resistance Levels
- Look for swing highs/lows and psychological price points
- Round numbers (00, 50 pips in forex; round dollars in futures)
- Confluent levels across multiple timeframes = strongest
- S/R are ZONES, not exact points

### Market Context and Risk Management
- Trending markets -> breakout strategies with wider stops
- Consolidating markets -> reversal patterns within ranges, tighter stops
- Limit risk to 1-2% per trade

## Main Strategies

### 1. Pin Bar Strategy
- Long tail/wick + small body = rejection signal
- Bullish pin bar: long lower tail (sellers pushed down, buyers regained control)
- Bearish pin bar: long upper tail
- Entry: buy above high of bullish pin bar, sell below low of bearish pin bar
- Stop loss: just beyond the tail
- Target: next significant S/R level

### 2. Inside Bar Strategy
- Second candle fully contained within mother bar's range
- Reflects consolidation/indecision before breakout
- Best on daily charts (less noise)
- Tighter consolidation = stronger signal
- Trending markets: continuation pattern
- At S/R levels: potential reversal signal
- Multiple inside bars = prolonged consolidation = stronger breakout

### 3. Breakout and Retest Strategy
- Mark key levels tested multiple times
- Look for breakouts with strong momentum
- Wait for retest of broken level (former resistance becomes support)
- Watch for rejection patterns (pin bars, inside bars) at retest
- Enter in breakout direction, stop beyond retested level
- Target: next major S/R or height of prior consolidation

## Advanced Techniques

### Advanced Pattern Recognition
- **Engulfing patterns**: Bullish/bearish candle completely covers previous candle's body
- **Double tops/bottoms**: Classic reversal patterns at key turning points
- **Trendline channels**: Connect swing highs/lows with parallel lines

### Combining with Indicators (Minimal)
- Moving averages (20/50-period) as dynamic S/R
- Bollinger Bands for overbought/oversold in ranging markets
- **Golden rule**: Price action leads, indicators confirm

## Chart Setup
- Plain candlestick chart, neutral colors
- Mark only critical S/R levels (horizontal lines over trendlines)
- Minimal indicators
- Color coding: one for support, one for resistance, one for entries/exits
- Three-chart setup: daily (context), 4H (setups), 1H (entries)
