# How To Find and Profit From Trapped Traders

By Galen Woods - 7 min read

Trapped traders offer a sound premise for a trading setup. With two simple guidelines, 
you can identify bear and bull traps and exploit them in your trades.

## Core Concept

One of the best ways to time your market entry is to locate trapped traders. When these 
traders realize they are wrong, they will try to get out of the market and reverse their 
positions. In doing so, they offer us an advantage in our entry timing.

An example is the Hikkake pattern, which takes advantage of traders who went in on the 
wrong side of an Inside Bar.

### Bear Trap
- Market breaks out below an inside bar to lure bearish traders in
- Reverses upwards
- Bearish traders forced to exit short positions
- Go long to exploit the market flow

### Bull Trap
- Market breaks out above a level to lure bullish traders
- Reverses downwards
- Bullish traders forced to exit long positions
- Go short to exploit the market flow

## Two Guidelines for Finding Trapped Traders

### #1: A rush towards a support or resistance level
- Look for a market rush towards a support or resistance level
- This rush should get traders excited and hopeful
- Should look solid and credible
- Watch for: Consecutive Trend Bars, Gaps, Outside Bars

### #2: The level quickly rejects the attempt to break it
- Look for swift, sudden, and possibly repeated rejections
- Watch for: Pin Bars, Two-Bar Reversals, Failure to close beyond S/R level

## Four Examples

1. **Bull Trap - FDAX 5-Minute (Futures)**: Bearish outside bar with no follow-through, 
   bullish outside bar took over, then rejected at resistance (Double Top) with a Pin Bar

2. **Bear Trap - EURUSD 4-Hour (Forex)**: Multiple signs of trapped bears - bullish inside 
   bar, failures to close below support, bullish pin bar, bullish reversal bar

3. **Bull Trap - BAC Daily (Stocks)**: Sharp upswing + bullish gap bar through resistance, 
   immediately rejected with climactic bearish outside bar/pin bar

4. **Bear Trap - BTC/USD Hourly (Crypto)**: Five-bar downthrust breached support, quickly 
   rejected with bullish thrust, then tested again with bullish pin bar

## The Approach in a Nutshell
- Don't think about when to enter the market
- Think about when other traders would enter, and when they would become anxious
- Enter the market as it moves against them
