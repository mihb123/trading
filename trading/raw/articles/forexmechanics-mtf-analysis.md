Multi-timeframe analysis (MTF) gives three things: directional context, level hierarchy, and more precise entries with tighter stops. The higher timeframe (W1, D1) shows trend and structure, middle shows setup, lower (H4, H1) provides entry trigger.

## Top-Down Principle
Start from highest relevant timeframe and work down. W1 identifies dominant trend and major S/R zones for direction. D1 looks for structure inside that trend (impulse or correction, approaching support or resistance). H4/H1 provides precise entry via candlestick patterns, pin bars, RSI divergence at D1 levels.

HTF answers 'which way', LTF answers 'exactly when'.

## Elder's Triple Screen System
Alexander Elder formalised MTF analysis in the 1980s. Three screens:
- Screen 1 (W1) — Directional filter: determine trend direction only. Weekly MACD Histogram or visual read.
- Screen 2 (D1) — Correction: catch pullbacks counter to Screen 1 trend. Use oscillator (Force Index, Stochastic, RSI) in oversold during W1 uptrend.
- Screen 3 (H4/H1) — Entry timing: wait for reversal patterns (engulfing, pin bar, morning star) at the zone identified by Screen 2.

## Timeframe Selection
4-6x gap between adjacent timeframes. Common sets:
- MN/W1/D1 — Position trading (weeks-months)
- W1/D1/H4 — Swing trading (days-weeks)
- D1/H4/H1 — Short swing/day trading
- H4/H1/M15 — Day trading
- H1/M15/M5 — Scalping

## Conflicts Between Timeframes
Conflict is normal state. Rules:
1. HTF usually provides directional filter
2. No setup = no trade (lack of confluence = impulse-driven decision)
3. Consolidation on HTF is toughest scenario
4. Divergence between TFs can be early warning
5. Decide SL management TF BEFORE opening position

## Common Mistakes
1. Bottom-up analysis (starting from M15/H1)
2. Too many timeframes (5-6 = paralysis)
3. Trading against the HTF
4. Switching timeframes mid-trade
5. Ignoring session context on lower TFs
6. Trading in thin windows/around rollover

## Workflow for Swing Trader (W1/D1/H4)
1. W1 — once a week (weekend). Identify trend, draw S/R zones.
2. D1 — every morning. Check approach to W1 zones, correction depth, oscillators.
3. H4 — 2-3 times daily. Look for entry signal if D1 identified zone.
4. Execution: SL below H4 pattern low or D1 S/R zone. TP at next D1 S/R zone.

## Pre-Click Checklist
Eight questions before pressing BUY/SELL:
1. HTF direction agree with position?
2. LTF gave actual trigger (candlestick pattern, break of level)?
3. Time of day — LDN-NY overlap or thin Asian window?
4. Macro calendar — tier-1 event within 2h (day trade) / 48h (swing)?
5. Spread and slippage — will R:R survive real costs?
6. Exit plan — SL in pips/price, TP, management at 1R?
7. Position size — calculated from SL distance and % capital at risk?
8. Invalidation condition — what kills thesis before SL is touched?
