## Why Day Trade GBP/USD?

### Benefits
- Volatility: GBP/USD can be more volatile than some other major pairs. During news shocks or thin-liquidity periods, spreads can widen.
- Relative safety: Two of the most modern economies. London is the world's largest FX trading centre, with UK accounting for about 38% of global FX turnover.
- Diverse trading vehicles: GBP/USD is among the most traded currency pairs globally. BIS Triennial Survey: 7.6% of global turnover.
- Manoeuvrability: Sizeable number of pips in a single move compared to other major pairs. Ideal for breakout trading.

### Drawbacks & Risks
- Rapid movement: can lose money quickly. Need discipline with risk management.
- Report heavy: responds significantly to UK economic reports.
- Ambiguity: volatility often results in false signals and breakouts.
- Automated competition: algorithmic trading plays major role in modern FX markets.

### Influences on Movement
- Economic growth: When US economy looks stronger than UK's, dollar rises against pound.
- Political events: Major elections can have noticeable impact on rates.
- Monetary policy: Fed and BoE actions seriously influence rates.

### Currency Correlations
Because currencies are priced in pairs, no single pair is totally independent. GBP/JPY is a cross rate mathematically related to GBP/USD and USD/JPY. Correlation ranges from -1 to +1. Over time correlations can change due to monetary policy and economic factors.

## Day Trading GBP/USD Strategy

### Timing
Best window: when both UK and US markets are open. GBP/USD liquidity is often highest during London-New York overlap (roughly 13:00-17:00 GMT, shifting with daylight saving). Spreads are usually tightest when liquidity is highest.

### Trading Breakouts Strategy
Look for solid risk-reward ratios. Example: risking 25 pips, aiming for 100 pips (1:4). Don't risk more than 1-2% of account balance per trade.

### Trading News
Economic data on UK unemployment rate, manufacturing growth, consumer sentiment affect the market. Monitor BLS Employment Situation (NFP) and CPI. Secondary sources: Google Finance, Yahoo Finance, DailyFX, Bloomberg.

### 4-Hour GBP/USD Strategy
Use 4-hour and daily chart. Daily timeframe identifies main trend, 4-hour for entering positions.

Indicators: Slow stochastic (13, 5, 5) on both charts. EMA 4, EMA 14, EMA 50 on 4-hour chart.

Selling Rules: Once 4 EMA crosses 50 EMA + 14 EMA to downside on 4-hour → sell at market. Stop loss 50 pips. Take profit 150 pips (1:3).

Buying Rules: Once 4 EMA crosses 50 EMA + 14 EMA to upside on 4-hour → buy at market. Stop loss 50 pips. Take profit 150 pips.

## Role of the Great British Pound
Despite small size, UK has one of world's largest economies. London is the FX capital. UK remains one of Europe's largest hydrocarbon producers.

## Role of the US Dollar
- Most prevalent currency in popular pairs
- World's second-largest trading nation after China
- Some countries peg currencies to USD
- Gold and commodities priced in USD
- Crude oil primarily priced and traded in USD
- World's reserve currency: BIS estimates USD on one side of 89.2% of forex trades
- Giant global banks hold substantial percentage of reserves in USD

Economic indicators to watch: Retail Sales, Trade Balance, NFP, ISM Manufacturing, PPI, Industrial Production, CPI, ISM Non-Manufacturing, Federal Reserve Minutes.

## FAQ

Best time: London-New York overlap (roughly 13:00-17:00 UK time). Outside overlaps, liquidity thinner and spreads wider.

Worst times: First or last day of week. Monday has low volatility, fewer opportunities. Friday has over-weekend risk.

Daily movement: Varies significantly by market regime. Estimate using ATR over chosen lookback period.

Is it profitable? High volatility and deep liquidity make it one of the most suitable day trading pairs.
