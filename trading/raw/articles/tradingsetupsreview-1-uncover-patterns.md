# How To Uncover Price Action Trading Patterns From Scratch

By Galen Woods - 8 min read

This guide shows you how to unearth powerful price action trading patterns and build up your understanding of market price action to help you trade better.

## Key Points

- No one has a monopoly on price action trading patterns - they belong to no one
- Price action trading patterns are nothing mysterious, but require hard work and many hours studying charts
- Focus on simple price patterns spanning 2-3 price bars/candlesticks

## The 4-Step Process

### Step 1: Getting The Basics
- Open a blank candlestick chart
- Adjust zoom to show ~200 candlesticks
- Use standard candlestick chart (not Heiken-Ashi, Renko)
- No indicators loaded
- Recommended platforms: NinjaTrader or TradingView

### Step 2: Focus With A Bias
- Draw a line connecting first candlestick to last candlestick
- Line slants up = bullish bias (only interested in buying)
- Line slants down = bearish bias (only interested in selling)
- Line horizontal = look for entries in both directions

### Step 3: Find Ideal Entry Points
- Circle turning points you would ideally buy or sell at
- Consider: holding period, reward-to-risk ratio, acceptable adverse excursion
- No correct answer - depends on personal preferences

### Step 4: Study For Similarities
- Distill observations from highlighted price action
- Work with OHLC data: Is candle bullish/bearish? What is the range? How does it compare to previous?
- Look for Trend Bars, Dojis, gaps between candlesticks

## Example: MSFT Daily Chart (Bullish)
- Each formation had a bearish down bar (lower high and lower low)
- Two formations had a bullish gap before takeoff
- All involved a bearish Trend Bar followed by a bullish Trend Bar

## Example: LL Stock (Bearish)
- Bearish gaps confirming entries
- Outside bars (higher high and lower low)
- Range expansion at turning points

## Conclusion
- Develop patterns into complete trading strategies with entry/exit and risk management
- Consider backtesting with code
- Analyze market context for pattern validity
- Move beyond OHLC to volume patterns
