Price Action Trading - Nial Fuller (PriceAction.com)

Definition: Price action trading is a methodology for financial market speculation which consists of the analysis of basic price movement across time. It's used by retail traders, institutional traders, and hedge fund managers.

Key Concepts:
- Price action ignores fundamental factors and looks primarily at price history
- It's a "pure" form of technical analysis with no second-hand, price-derived indicators
- Traders focus on the last 3-6 months of price action, with lighter focus on distant history
- Price history includes swing highs/lows and support/resistance levels
- "Keep It Simple Stupid" (KISS) - clean charts, no indicators, no news

Three Core Patterns:
1. Inside Bar: Two-bar pattern, inside bar contained within mother bar's range. Used as breakout pattern in trends, reversal at key levels.
2. Pin Bar: Single candlestick showing price rejection/reversal. Works in trends, ranges, and counter-trend. Tail shows rejection direction.
3. Fakey Pattern: False breakout of an inside bar. Market breaks out briefly then reverses back within mother bar range. Works with trends, against trends, in ranges.

Confluence: The best signals form at "confluent" points where multiple factors align (trend + support level + price action signal). More confluence = higher probability.
