## Introduction
Top-down analysis is the practice of aligning multiple timeframes to create high-probability setups. Too often, traders enter based on a single timeframe pattern and get stopped out because they ignored the bigger picture.

## Why Multiple Timeframe Analysis Matters
When you align all your timeframes in the same direction, you get price expansion that creates winning trades. A bullish order block on 5-minute means nothing if the daily chart shows bearish expansion.

## Establish a Higher Timeframe Bias
Start with daily, weekly, or monthly. Look for swing highs/lows, sweeps of liquidity, candle closures. Determine if HTF will expand higher, lower, or consolidate.

Example: If daily swept a previous high and closed back inside range with bearish engulfing, bias is bearish.

## Define Structure on Intermediate Timeframe
Drop to 4H or 1H. Confirm HTF bias by checking Change in State of Delivery (CISD). Identify points of interest aligned with HTF bias. Wait for reaction, candle closure, or protected swing.

## Refine Entry on Lower Timeframe
15M or 5M. Following intermediate TF reaction, look for CISD confirming alignment. All three timeframes should point same direction. Seek entries following new protected lows or continuation order blocks. Stops beyond protected high/low, target HTF objectives.

## Bearish Example
Daily: expansion lower, retracement up, consolidation. Sweep of consolidation high, bearish daily closure. Hourly: price retraces into lower half of previous day's range. 5M: wait for CISD forming protected high, enter using continuation OBs.

## Bullish Example
Daily: bullish trend, expansion higher, retrace into FVG, protected swing. Price takes out high, retraces into new FVG. Bullish daily closure. Hourly: CISD at reversal point, FVG in upper half. Entry on new protected swing. Result: 2R trade.

## Core Principle
Expansion occurs when all timeframes align: HTF sets direction, intermediate provides structure, LTF delivers precision entries.
