# Swing Trading Strategy with Multiple Time-Frames

Using multiple time-frames in your analysis is a solid method to find high probability trades. This is a simple dual time-frame method for swing trading using SMA 20 slope divergence.

## Core Concept
The daily time-frame is the primary window of analysis. The weekly chart offers the big picture view. The slope of the 20-period SMA identifies the trend in both time-frames.

The key insight: we look for DIVERGENCE between the two time-frames' SMA slopes. When weekly is bullish (SMA slope positive) but daily turns bearish (SMA slope negative), that's a pullback opportunity for a long entry.

## Bullish Rules
1. Weekly SMA slope turns positive (bullish bias change)
2. Wait for daily SMA slope to turn negative (pullback opportunity)
3. Place buy stop above the lowest swing high above daily SMA
4. Cancel if weekly SMA starts sloping down

## Bearish Rules
1. Weekly SMA slope turns negative
2. Wait for daily SMA slope to turn positive (pullback opportunity)
3. Place sell stop below the highest swing low below daily SMA
4. Cancel if weekly SMA starts sloping up

## Key Insights
- This strategy's value is also in keeping you OUT of the market during congestion
- On losing trades, watch for price action warnings - weekly bars closing entirely above SMA is a bullish sign
- Can be extended with monthly TF for position trading or hourly for tighter entries
- Dual TF divergence gives precise, rules-based entries without subjectivity
